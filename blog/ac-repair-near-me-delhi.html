How to Find a Genuine AC Repair Technician Near You

Check for a Physical Business Address

Any genuine AC repair business operates from a fixed address — a workshop, office, or registered location. This is not about having a fancy office; it is about accountability. A business with no verifiable address has no accountability if they do a poor job. Air Control operates from Sant Nagar, East of Kailash, New Delhi — verifiable, established, and in business since 1987.

Look at Google Reviews (Not Just the Rating)

A 4.8 rating with 12 reviews and a 4.3 rating with 600 reviews tell very different stories. Read the content of reviews, not just the stars. Look for mentions of: accurate diagnosis, transparent pricing, punctual arrival, and whether the problem stayed fixed. Be sceptical of businesses with suddenly appearing bursts of reviews with identical language.

Ask for a Written Quote Before Any Work

A professional technician diagnoses the problem, tells you what needs doing and why, and provides a cost estimate before lifting a tool. If anyone refuses to quote a price before starting, or insists on opening the unit before telling you the price, politely end the conversation. Air Control's technicians always provide a verbal and written diagnosis before starting any repair.

Verify the Technician Carries Proper Equipment

A trained AC technician arrives with: a manifold pressure gauge set (for gas diagnosis), a multimeter (for electrical testing), leak detection tools, a full toolkit, and the common spare parts (capacitors, contactors, drain pipe). If a technician arrives with just a screwdriver, that is a warning sign.

6 Red Flags — Avoid These Technicians

Red Flag 1: Quotes ₹200–₹300 for "service" but charges ₹3,000+ on the day without clear explanation.

Red Flag 2: Immediately says your compressor needs replacement without performing a proper pressure test or electrical diagnosis.

Red Flag 3: Demands full payment in advance before beginning work.

Red Flag 4: Cannot explain what they found or what they did in plain language.

Red Flag 5: Does not provide a receipt, invoice, or any written warranty for the repair.

Red Flag 6: Has no fixed business address or traceable business identity — only a WhatsApp number.

Questions to Ask Before Booking AC Repair

  1. "Can you give me a written quote after diagnosis before starting any work?" — A yes here is non-negotiable.
  2. "What brand and type of refrigerant do you use, and do you carry a manifold gauge set?" — Tests whether they are genuinely equipped.
  3. "What warranty do you provide on the repair?" — Air Control provides a 90-day repair warranty. Any serious business provides at least 30 days.
  4. "Are your technicians direct employees or contractors?" — Direct employees are more accountable and consistently trained.
  5. "Can I pay after the work is done and I verify it is working?" — Professional businesses agree to this; fly-by-night operators do not.
